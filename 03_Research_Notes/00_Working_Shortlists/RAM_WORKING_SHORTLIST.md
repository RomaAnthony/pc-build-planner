# RAM Working Shortlist

Date: 2026-06-12

This is the temporary working note for choosing RAM. It should stay short and be updated or deleted as we learn more.

## Build Target

- Capacity: 64GB total.
- Layout: 2x32GB only.
- Speed: DDR5-6000.
- Latency: CL30 preferred, CL32 acceptable if the deal is clearly better.
- Platform: AM5, Ryzen 7 9700X baseline, possible stronger Ryzen if budget allows.
- Preference: AMD EXPO kit when possible.
- Avoid: 4x16GB kits, CL40 unless extremely cheap, random untrusted sellers.

## 6000 vs 6400

We should not chase DDR5-6400 for this build.

DDR5-6000 is still the clean AM5 choice, especially with 2x32GB. DDR5-6400 can work on some CPUs and boards, but it adds more memory-controller risk and may need tuning. For SQL, finance work, productivity, and light gaming, the real gain is too small to be worth extra instability or price.

Decision:

- Best target: 6000 CL30 EXPO.
- Good fallback: 6000 CL32 EXPO if meaningfully cheaper or from a much better seller.
- Accept only with strong discount: 6000 CL36.
- Avoid for our main shortlist: 6000 CL40, 6400 kits, 4-stick kits.

## Future 128GB Note

Adding another 2x32GB later is possible, but not guaranteed to run at 6000 CL30 on AM5. Four DDR5 sticks often need lower speed or looser timings.

For now, 64GB as 2x32GB is the right choice. If 128GB becomes likely soon, we should rethink the plan before buying.

## Corsair Answer

Corsair is not a bad brand. The problem is value in the captured Hungary and Poland pages.

The cheaper Corsair kits are mostly CL40 or CL36, so they are not ideal for our target. The Corsair kits that match our target better, especially CL30, look much more expensive than Kingston, Patriot, GOODRAM, G.Skill, and sometimes Silicon Power.

So the current decision is:

- Do not ban Corsair.
- Do not pay extra just for Corsair.
- Only keep Corsair if a 2x32GB 6000 CL30 or CL32 EXPO kit appears at a strong price from a good seller.

### RGB vs Non-RGB Pricing

Usually RGB costs a little more, but market pricing does not always behave logically.

Captured examples:

- Poland Kingston White RGB CL30: 3,417.77 PLN, about 804 EUR.
- Poland Kingston Black non-RGB EXPO CL30: 3,635.50 PLN, about 855 EUR.
- Hungary Kingston RGB CL30: 340,050 HUF, about 958 EUR.
- Hungary Kingston Black non-RGB EXPO CL30 clean option from foramax: 339,890 HUF, about 957 EUR.

So in Poland, the captured RGB/white kit is cheaper than the black non-RGB kit. In Hungary, they are basically the same if we ignore the unclear cheapest marketplace listing.

Conclusion:

- Do not assume non-RGB is cheaper.
- For RAM, color/RGB is secondary. Exact spec, EXPO, seller, warranty, and final price matter more.
- If RGB is cheaper and the model is correct, it is fine to buy RGB. We can turn lights off or set a static color.
