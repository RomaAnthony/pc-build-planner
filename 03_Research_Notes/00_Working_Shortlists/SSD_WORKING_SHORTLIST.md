## Current Recommendation

Buy a 2TB PCIe 4.0 NVMe TLC SSD as the main system/work/database drive.

Do not pay a big premium for PCIe 5.0 yet. For this build, Gen5 is mostly heat and price unless a very strong model lands within about 10-15% of a good Gen4 option.

Do not buy 1TB unless the budget becomes very tight. Windows, tools, databases, projects, cache, games, and future growth make 2TB the practical sweet spot.

## PCIe 4 vs PCIe 5

PCIe 4 is the right default.

Reasons:
- Strong Gen4 drives already reach around 7,000-7,450 MB/s sequential reads.
- The user's work is database/SQL/finance/productivity, not constant 100GB+ scratch-file editing.
- Gen5 drives can run hotter and often need stronger heatsinking.
- Money is better saved for RTX 5080, PSU, motherboard, case airflow, or CPU.

PCIe 5 is only interesting if:
- A proven 2TB Gen5 TLC drive is within about 10-15% of KC3000 / SN850X / 990 PRO.
- It fits cleanly under the motherboard M.2 heatsink.
- Reviews show no annoying heat/throttling behavior.

## 1TB vs 2TB

2TB is the correct main-drive size.

Reasons:
- 1TB becomes tight after Windows, tools, SQL databases, project files, cache, and games.
- Larger SSDs often have better sustained write behavior and endurance than smaller versions.
- Replacing the main SSD later is annoying compared with buying the right size once.

4TB is optional later, not needed now. Add it as a second drive if storage actually becomes a problem.

## Market Direction

Hungary and Poland are both fine for SSDs.

- Hungary: better local warranty route if the price is close.
- Poland: good if bundling with RAM/GPU from x-kom or another trusted store.
- Ukraine: not worth using for SSD unless the price is clearly much better after warranty/logistics risk.

Because SSDs are small and low-shipping-risk, Poland is acceptable. But if Hungary is within roughly 5-8% on a trusted seller, local warranty simplicity wins.
