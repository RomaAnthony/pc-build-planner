Date: 2026-06-12

This is the temporary working note for RTX 5080 research. It should stay short and decision-focused.

## Build Context

- CPU baseline: Ryzen 7 9700X, maybe Ryzen 9 if budget allows.
- RAM: 64GB DDR5-6000 CL30.
- SSD: 2TB Gen4 NVMe.
- PSU target: 1000W ATX 3.1 / PCIe 5.1 with native 12V-2x6.
- Case target: black glass airflow case, likely Corsair 3500X style or similar.
- Monitor context: LG UltraGear 32GS95UV-B.AEU 32-inch flat 4K 240Hz FreeSync OLED as the main display, plus 4K 60Hz and 1080p 144Hz secondary displays.
- Buy timing: build PC first, buy GPU later when price/seller/warranty are right.

Monitor implication:
- The RTX 5080 target is justified by the main monitor. A 32-inch 4K 240Hz OLED can actually show the benefit of a high-end GPU.
- This does not mean buying the GPU immediately. It means the build should stay ready for a strong GPU later: good PSU, airflow, GPU clearance, and native 16-pin/12V-2x6 support.
- For productivity/database work, the PC is useful before the GPU. For gaming on the OLED, the later RTX 5080 becomes the real unlock.

## Decision Logic

We are not choosing the absolute cheapest RTX 5080 automatically.

Goal:

- Best long-term value.
- Good cooler, normal card design, and trusted warranty route.
- Avoid paying for expensive branding when performance is basically the same.
- Avoid compact/SFF/dual-fan designs unless they are clearly good value and fit the case/cooling plan.
- Avoid weak seller/warranty situations even if the headline price is low.

Used Hungarian market benchmark:

- Excellent used deal: clearly below 430,000 HUF.
- Maybe worth it: 430,000-450,000 HUF, only with transferable warranty and trusted seller.
- Not worth used risk: 450,000 HUF or more, because new value cards are already around 462,000-480,000 HUF.
